SECTION 2: FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
=======================================

Q: How much does a bail bond cost?
A: Our bondsman will explain all the options and pricing when they call you back. Every case is different.

Q: Can bail be reduced?
A: Yes. An attorney can file a motion to reduce bail if it is excessive or circumstances change. We can recommend attorneys if needed.

Q: Can I get my premium back?
A: No. The premium is a non-refundable service fee earned by the bondsman. This is standard across all bail bond agencies in Florida per Florida Statute 648.

Q: What if I can't afford the full premium upfront?
A: We offer flexible payment plans. Our bondsman will explain all options when they call you back.

Q: How long does release take?
A: Typical release times are 2-8 hours after we post the bond, depending on the jail and time of day. Some counties are faster than others. Lee County averages 2-4 hours. Collier is often faster at 1-2 hours.

Q: What is the difference between bail and a bond?
A: Bail is the full amount set by the court that guarantees the defendant will appear. A bail bond is a service where a bail bond agent posts the full bail on your behalf, and you pay a percentage as a premium.

Q: Can anyone be denied bail?
A: Yes. Under Florida Statute 903, bail can be denied for capital offenses (where proof of guilt is evident), extreme flight risks, or when the defendant poses a danger to the community. Only a judge can deny bail.

Q: What do I need to get someone out of jail?
A: You'll need: (1) The defendant's full name, (2) The county/jail they're in, (3) A valid government-issued ID, (4) The bond amount (if known — we can look it up), and (5) A way to pay the premium (cash, card, or payment plan).

Q: What are your hours?
A: We are available 24/7, 365 days a year. Call us anytime at (239) 332-2245.

Q: Do you cover my county?
A: Yes! Shamrock covers all 67 Florida counties through our statewide network of licensed agents.

Q: How does the bail process work?
A: Step 1 - Call us. Step 2 - We gather the defendant's information. Step 3 - A cosigner signs the bond paperwork (can be done digitally on your phone). Step 4 - We post the bond at the jail. Step 5 - The defendant is released (usually 2-8 hours). Step 6 - The defendant must appear at all court dates.

Q: What happens if the defendant misses court?
A: Under Florida Statute 903.26, if the defendant fails to appear, the bond is forfeited and a bench warrant is issued. The cosigner becomes financially responsible for the full bond amount. That's why court dates are critical.

Q: What is a cosigner/indemnitor?
A: A cosigner (also called an indemnitor) is the person who guarantees the defendant will appear in court. They are financially responsible if the defendant doesn't show up. Under Florida law, the indemnitor signs an agreement accepting this responsibility.

Q: Can I bail someone out from another state?
A: Yes! We can handle everything remotely. You can sign paperwork from your phone no matter where you are. Our agent will post the bond locally in Florida.

Q: What is a first appearance hearing?
A: A first appearance hearing is a court proceeding where the judge reviews the charges, sets or confirms bail, and appoints a public defender if the defendant cannot afford an attorney. This typically happens within 24 hours of arrest.

Q: What happens to collateral after the case is over?
A: Under Florida Statute 648.442, collateral must be returned within a reasonable time after the bond obligation is discharged, provided all fees and premiums have been paid.

Q: Do you accept credit cards?
A: Yes. We accept all major credit cards, debit cards, cash, and payment plans. We can send you a secure payment link right to your phone.

SECTION 3: BAIL BOND PAPERWORK DESCRIPTIONS
=============================================

When you bail someone out, you'll sign these documents electronically on your phone via our secure SignNow system. No need to visit our office — everything can be done digitally while on the phone:

1. APPEARANCE BOND
   The official court document that guarantees the defendant will appear at all scheduled court hearings. This is filed directly with the court.

2. INDEMNITY AGREEMENT
   Your promise (as the cosigner) to pay the full bond amount if the defendant fails to appear in court. This is the most important document — it means you are financially responsible.

3. DISCLOSURE FORM
   Required by Florida law (F.S. 648.44). Explains your rights and responsibilities as a cosigner, including the non-refundable nature of the premium and your obligations.

4. SURETY TERMS AND CONDITIONS
   The rules of the bond — what the defendant must and must not do while out on bond (appear at court, not leave the state without permission, etc.).

5. PROMISSORY NOTE (if on a payment plan)
   If you are financing the premium, this document outlines your payment schedule, amounts due, and what happens if you miss a payment.

6. PREMIUM/COLLATERAL RECEIPT
   Documents any premium payments made and any collateral pledged (property, vehicles, etc.).

7. SSA RELEASE / WAIVER
   Standard release and waiver forms required for processing.

8. DEFENDANT APPLICATION
   Application form the defendant completes with personal information, employment, and references.

All documents are sent to your phone or email for electronic signature. You do not need to come to our office to sign — everything can be done from your phone while we're on the call together.

SECTION 5: FLORIDA BOND SCHEDULE RULES
=========================================

Florida does not have a single statewide bond schedule. Each county's Chief Judge issues a local Administrative Order that sets standard bond amounts for most offenses. Key rules:

GENERAL BOND GUIDELINES:
- Misdemeanors: Typically $500-$5,000 (varies by charge severity and county)
- Third-Degree Felonies: Typically $2,500-$15,000
- Second-Degree Felonies: Typically $5,000-$50,000
- First-Degree Felonies: Typically $10,000-$250,000+
- Life/Capital Felonies: No bond or judge-set only (no standard schedule)

COMMON CHARGE EXAMPLES:
- DUI (1st offense): $500-$1,500
- DUI (2nd or more): $1,000-$5,000
- Domestic Battery: $1,500-$5,000 (often requires magistrate hearing)
- Petit Theft / Shoplifting: $500-$1,000
- Grand Theft: $2,500-$10,000
- Possession of Cannabis (<20g): $500-$1,000
- Possession of Controlled Substance: $2,500-$15,000
- Aggravated Battery: $15,000-$50,000
- Robbery: $25,000-$100,000
- Burglary (Structure): $10,000-$50,000
- Burglary (Dwelling): $25,000-$100,000+
- Sexual Battery: Judge-set only (no standard bond)
- Murder: No bond / judge-set only
- Violation of Probation: Often no bond until VOP hearing

IMPORTANT NOTES ON BONDS:
- Bond amounts are SET BY THE JUDGE. The above are typical ranges, not guarantees.
- Each charge gets a SEPARATE bond amount per F.S. 903.046
- "No bond" means the defendant must wait for a judge to set bail at first appearance
- Domestic violence charges often require a mandatory hold until first appearance
- Some charges require special conditions (GPS monitoring, no contact orders, etc.)
- Bond amounts can be reduced or increased at the judge's discretion

SECTION 6: FLORIDA STATUTE 648 — BAIL BOND AGENTS
=============================================
(Chapter 648, Florida Statutes — Bail Bond Regulatory Law)

PURPOSE: Governs the licensing, conduct, and regulation of all bail bond agents in Florida.

KEY PROVISIONS SHANNON SHOULD KNOW:

6.1 LICENSING REQUIREMENTS (F.S. 648.34)
- Must be at least 18 years old
- Must be a U.S. citizen or legal alien with work authorization
- Must be a Florida resident
- Must hold a high school diploma or equivalent
- Must complete a 120-hour professional certification course
- Must pass a certification exam (score 80% or higher)
- Must undergo a background check through the Department of Justice
- Licensed and regulated by the Florida Department of Financial Services

6.2 PROHIBITED ACTS (F.S. 648.44)
- CANNOT suggest or advise the employment of any particular attorney
- CANNOT solicit business in jails, courthouses, or on court/jail property
- CANNOT initiate solicitations at a detainee's or family's residence between 9:00 PM and 8:00 AM
- CANNOT wear or display identification other than department-issued license at correctional facilities
- CANNOT sign or countersign blank bonds
- CANNOT allow unlicensed individuals to execute bonds
- CANNOT charge fees outside the approved rate

6.3 PREMIUM AND FINANCIAL RULES (F.S. 648.44)
- The premium is NON-REFUNDABLE once the bond is executed
- No fees, expenses, or charges can be deducted from collateral
- All funds received must be accurately reported and accounted for
- Collateral must be returned when the bond obligation is discharged (F.S. 648.442)
- Build-up funds pledged to indemnify an insurer must be properly managed

6.4 AGENCY REQUIREMENTS (F.S. 648.382, 648.387)
- A person cannot own or control a bail bond agency unless they are a licensed and appointed bail bond agent
- Each agency location must designate a primary bail bond agent in charge
- Insurers must have a managing general agent in Florida to supervise operations
- Licensees must maintain detailed records of all bonds executed

6.5 PENALTIES (F.S. 648.571, 648.572)
- General violations: Second-degree misdemeanor
- Serious infractions (aiding unlicensed persons, operating after felony conviction): Third-degree felony
- The Department of Financial Services can suspend or revoke licenses

SECTION 7: FLORIDA STATUTE 903 — BAIL LAW
=============================================
(Chapter 903, Florida Statutes — The Bail System)

PURPOSE: Establishes the constitutional and statutory framework for the bail system in Florida, including rules for setting bail, bond forfeiture, and pretrial release.

KEY PROVISIONS SHANNON SHOULD KNOW:

7.1 PURPOSE OF BAIL (F.S. 903.046)
- To ensure the defendant's appearance at all court proceedings
- To protect the community from unreasonable danger
- Each charge must have a SEPARATE and SPECIFIC bail amount
- Only a JUDGE can set, reduce, or modify bail

7.2 TYPES OF BAIL (F.S. 903.011-903.09)
- CASH BOND: Full bail amount deposited with the court. Refunded when case concludes (minus fees).
- SURETY BOND: A bail bond agent and surety company post the bond. Defendant pays a premium (non-refundable).
- RECOGNIZANCE (ROR): Release without monetary obligation, based on promise to appear.
- PRETRIAL RELEASE: Release with conditions (check-ins, curfew, GPS, etc.) — requires court approval.

7.3 CRITERIA FOR SETTING BAIL (F.S. 903.046)
The court considers:
- Nature and circumstances of the offense
- Weight of the evidence against the defendant
- Defendant's ties to the community (family, employment, length of residency)
- Financial ability to post bail
- Defendant's history of court appearances
- Danger the defendant poses to the community
- Source of funds used to post bail
- Whether the defendant is already on probation, parole, or other release

7.4 BOND FORFEITURE (F.S. 903.26, 903.27)
- Bond is FORFEITED if the defendant fails to appear as required
- Court declares forfeiture and issues a bench warrant
- The surety (bail bond agent) has a specific timeframe to locate and surrender the defendant
- If the defendant is found and surrendered within the allowed period, the forfeiture may be discharged or reduced
- The COSIGNER is financially responsible for the FULL bail amount if forfeiture is finalized

7.5 SURETY'S RIGHT TO ARREST (F.S. 903.22)
- A surety (or bail bond agent) has the right to arrest the defendant BEFORE bond forfeiture for the purpose of surrendering them to the court
- This is how "bounty hunting" works — the agent finds and returns the defendant to custody

7.6 PRETRIAL RELEASE VIOLATIONS (F.S. 903.0471)
- If a defendant violates conditions of pretrial release, bail may be revoked
- Defendant may be held without bond until a hearing
- Violations include: new arrests, failure to check in, violating no-contact orders, failing drug tests

7.7 RESTRICTIONS ON BAIL (F.S. 903.133, 903.132)
- Capital felonies: NO bail if proof of guilt is evident or presumption is great
- Certain violent crimes after prior conviction: Judge may deny bail
- Probation/community control violators: May be held without bail pending hearing
