## Part 2: How Bail Is Set in Lee County

### The First Appearance Hearing

Under Florida law (Rule of Criminal Procedure 3.130), every person arrested in Florida must appear before a judge **within 24 hours of arrest**. This is called the **First Appearance hearing**.

At First Appearance, the judge will:
1. Inform the defendant of their charges
2. Advise them of their constitutional rights
3. Determine whether probable cause exists
4. **Set the bail amount and conditions**

Lee County First Appearance hearings are typically held at **8:30 AM and 1:30 PM daily** at the Lee County Justice Center.

> **Tip for Families:** If you know when the hearing is, watch it via the Lee County court livestream and call us immediately when the bond amount is announced. We can start the paperwork before the hearing ends.

### The Lee County Bail Schedule

For common misdemeanor offenses, Lee County uses a pre-set bond schedule that allows release without a First Appearance hearing. Examples:

| Charge | Typical Bond Amount |
|---|---|
| DUI (first offense) | $500 – $1,000 |
| Misdemeanor Battery | $500 |
| Petit Theft | $500 |
| Drug Possession (small amount) | $500 – $2,500 |
| Felony Battery | $5,000 – $15,000 |
| Aggravated Assault | $10,000 – $25,000 |
| Drug Trafficking | $50,000+ |

*These are general ranges. Actual bond amounts are set at judicial discretion.*

## Part 3: Bail Bond Costs in Lee County

### The Florida Regulated Rate

Florida law sets the bail bond premium at **10% of the total bail amount**, with a **$100 minimum per charge**. This rate is regulated by the Florida Department of Financial Services and cannot be legally discounted below 10%.

**Examples:**
- $1,000 bail → $100 premium (minimum)
- $5,000 bail → $500 premium
- $10,000 bail → $1,000 premium
- $25,000 bail → $2,500 premium
- $50,000 bail → $5,000 premium

## Part 5: What Happens After Release

### Court Appearances Are Mandatory

Once released on a bail bond, the defendant **must appear at every scheduled court date**. Failure to appear results in:
- Immediate bond revocation
- Issuance of an arrest warrant
- Financial liability for the full bail amount (on the indemnitor/co-signer)

### The Co-Signer's Responsibilities

The person who signs the bail bond agreement (the **indemnitor**) guarantees the defendant's court appearances. If the defendant fails to appear, the indemnitor is responsible for the full bail amount.

Indemnitors should:
- Ensure the defendant has transportation to court
- Keep track of all court dates
- Immediately notify Shamrock if the defendant cannot appear or violates any condition

### Shamrock's Court Reminder System

Shamrock Bail Bonds operates an automated court reminder system — defendants and indemnitors receive SMS reminders **7 days, 3 days, 1 day, and the morning of** each court date. This is one of many reasons our failure-to-appear rate is among the lowest in Southwest Florida.
