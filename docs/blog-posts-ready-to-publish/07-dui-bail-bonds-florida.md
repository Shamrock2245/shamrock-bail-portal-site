## What Happens After a DUI Arrest in Florida?

### 1. Booking and Chemical Test
After a DUI arrest, the defendant is taken to the county jail for booking. Under Florida's **implied consent law**, refusing a breathalyzer results in an automatic driver's license suspension (1 year first refusal, 18 months for a second). A blood or urine test may also be administered.

### 2. Mandatory Hold Period
Florida law requires that DUI arrestees be held until:
- They are **no longer under the influence** (typically at least 8 hours), OR
- A judge orders release at First Appearance

This mandatory hold means you generally **cannot post bail and secure immediate release** for a DUI arrest — you must wait for the hold period to expire or the First Appearance hearing.

### 3. First Appearance Hearing
The First Appearance hearing occurs within 24 hours of arrest. The judge reviews the charge, sets bail conditions (which may include an ignition interlock device requirement or prohibition on alcohol consumption), and formally advises the defendant of their rights.

## Conditions of Release After DUI

When a judge releases a DUI defendant on bail, conditions typically include:

- **No consumption of alcohol or controlled substances**
- **Random breathalyzer testing** in some cases
- **Ignition interlock device** required on all vehicles for high BAC or repeat offenders
- **No new arrests**
- **Surrender of passport** for out-of-state or foreign nationals with flight risk indicators
- **Reporting to a pretrial services officer** for higher-risk cases

Violating any of these conditions can result in **immediate bond revocation** and re-arrest.

## What Happens to the Driver's License After a DUI Arrest?

A Florida DUI arrest triggers **two separate license suspension events**:

1. **Administrative suspension (DHSMV):** Automatically begins 10 days after arrest unless challenged. First refusal = 1 year. First BAC ≥ 0.08 = 6 months.
2. **Criminal court suspension:** If convicted, additional suspension ordered by the court.

The defendant has **10 days from arrest** to request a formal review hearing from the Florida DHSMV to challenge the administrative suspension. This is a critical deadline — contact a DUI attorney immediately.

## DUI Bail Bonds: Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can someone drive themselves home after being released from DUI arrest?**  
No. Even if the mandatory hold period has expired, defendants should not drive themselves. Arrange a sober driver to pick them up from the jail.

**Q: Does posting bail for a DUI mean they admit guilt?**  
Absolutely not. Posting bail is a financial guarantee of court appearance only — it has no bearing on guilt or innocence.

**Q: Will a DUI show up on a background check after arrest?**  
An arrest record appears immediately upon booking. Whether a conviction appears depends on the case outcome. Charges that are dropped or result in acquittal can be expunged under Florida law.

**Q: What is the difference between DUI and DUBAL (Driving Under the Influence with Unlawful Blood Alcohol Level)?**  
In Florida, these are the same charge. A DUI conviction requires either impairment OR a BAC ≥ 0.08. Prosecutors may pursue either theory (or both).

**Q: Can Shamrock bail out someone arrested for DUI manslaughter?**  
Yes. DUI manslaughter is a first- or second-degree felony in Florida. Bond amounts are significantly higher, and a judge may impose special conditions. We handle all DUI charges, including serious felony DUI.
