## Chapter 648: The Florida Bail Bond Statute

**Florida Statutes Chapter 648** governs the bail bond industry throughout the state. Key provisions:

### Section 648.25 — License Requirement
Every person who writes, executes, or sells a bail bond in Florida must hold a **Bail Bond Agent license** issued by the Florida Department of Financial Services (DFS). You can verify a bondsman's license at [myfloridacfo.com](https://www.myfloridacfo.com).

### Section 648.357 — The Non-Negotiable Premium
The bail bond premium in Florida is **10% of the face amount of the bail bond**, with a **$100 minimum per charge**. This rate is set by law and cannot be:
- Discounted (no "8% bail bonds" — this is illegal)
- Waived
- Refunded after the bond is posted

If a bail bond agency offers you a rate below 10%, they are operating illegally. Report it to the Florida DFS.

### Section 648.44 — Transfer Fee
A **$125 transfer fee** may be charged for surety bonds in counties outside the home county of the surety insurer (the insurance company backing the bond). This fee:
- **Does NOT apply** to bonds in Lee County or Charlotte County for Shamrock
- **IS waived** for bonds over $25,000 (regardless of county)
- **Applies** to most other Florida counties

## Florida Constitution, Article I, Section 14 — The Right to Bail

Florida's Constitution guarantees the right to bail in most cases:

> "Unless charged with a capital offense or an offense punishable by life imprisonment and the proof of guilt is evident or the presumption is great, every person charged with a crime or violation of municipal or county ordinance shall be entitled to pretrial release on reasonable conditions."

This means:
- Bail cannot be set at an amount designed to be **deliberately unaffordable** (8th Amendment)
- Defendants with **strong community ties** and **low flight risk** must be given reasonable bail conditions
- Only capital and life felonies may be held without bail (and even then, only after a hearing)

## The First Appearance Requirement — Rule 3.130

Florida Rule of Criminal Procedure 3.130 requires that every arrested person appear before a judge **within 24 hours of arrest**. Failure to bring a defendant before a judge within 24 hours may entitle the defendant to release.

At First Appearance, the judge must:
1. Inform the defendant of the charges
2. Advise them of constitutional rights
3. Determine probable cause
4. Set bail or conditions of release

## What Happens When Bond Is Forfeited?

If a defendant fails to appear in court, the judge enters a **forfeiture order**. Under Florida law:

- The bondsman receives notice of the forfeiture
- The bondsman has **60 days** (extendable under certain circumstances) to locate and return the defendant to custody, or pay the full bond amount to the court
- If the defendant is returned to custody within the window, the forfeiture may be **vacated** (reversed)
- If the defendant is not found, the court collects the full bail amount from the bondsman and the bondsman pursues the indemnitor/collateral to recover

This is why bondsmen take co-signer obligations and collateral seriously — the financial stakes are real.

## The Bounty Hunter Law — Chapter 648.30

Florida law authorizes bail bond recovery agents (commonly called "bounty hunters") to locate and return defendants who have skipped bail. Key points:

- Must hold a **Limited Surety Agent license** from DFS
- Must notify local law enforcement before apprehending a defendant
- **Cannot enter a private residence** without permission or a warrant
- Use of force rules mirror peace officer standards

Unlicensed bounty hunters operating in Florida are committing a felony.

## Things That Are Illegal in Florida Bail Bonds

Be aware of these common violations of Florida bail bond law:

| Illegal Practice | What It Means |
|---|---|
| Discounting below 10% | Offering "8% bail" or "low-cost bail" — felony insurance fraud |
| Unlicensed bail bond writing | Writing bonds without a DFS license |
| Paying "runners" or "cappers" | Paying laypeople to bring in clients — illegal referral fees |
| Fee splitting with attorneys | Attorneys cannot receive referral fees from bondsmen |
| Falsifying indemnitor information | Knowingly accepting false information on bond applications |

If you believe a bail bond agent has violated Florida law, file a complaint with the Florida Department of Financial Services at [myfloridacfo.com](https://www.myfloridacfo.com) or call (850) 413-3089.
