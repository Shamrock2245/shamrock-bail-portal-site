## Part 3: What Slows Things Down (And How We Avoid It)

### Delay #1: Waiting for the First Appearance Hearing

If the defendant was just arrested and has not yet had their **First Appearance hearing**, bail has not been set yet. Under Florida law, this hearing must occur within **24 hours of arrest**.

**What you should do:** Call us immediately. We begin preparing paperwork before the hearing so we're ready to post the bond the moment the judge sets the amount.

### Delay #2: ICE Holds or Immigration Detainers

If federal immigration authorities (ICE) have placed a hold on the defendant, a standard bail bond will not release them. These situations require a **federal immigration bond** — a different process. Call us immediately if you suspect this applies.

### Delay #3: No-Bond Hold or Capital Offense

Certain charges in Florida are eligible for a no-bond hold — typically capital murder, certain sex offenses, or defendants deemed a danger to the public. In these cases, a hearing before a judge is required before bond can be set.

### Delay #4: Incomplete Paperwork or Information

Missing information — wrong date of birth, wrong spelling of name — can cause delays on both our end and at the jail. Have the defendant's ID information ready when you call.
